üì∫ Watching TV

The TV is not yet connected to the antenna, so it works with a Google Chromecast. The general Chromecast account is ours, but since many other guests use the TV please note that there might be other logged in accounts to Netflix, Prime Video or even YouTube.

If you have your own account, you can log to any of the paid streaming services and use the Chromecast with the white remote (this a guide to the Chromecast remote). If you do not have any, you can still watch YouTube, Italian public broadcast (RAI) and one private channel, called La7, through the Google Chromecast.

Watching RAI

RAI is the Italian public broadcast, like BBC for the UK, and it has multiple channels. RaiPlay is its on demand and streaming app, so you can watch both live and past broadcasts. While live broadcasts are almost uniquely in Italian, many foreign films do actually have the original version and sometimes even subtitles in languages different from Italian.

To watch RAI you just need to open the RaiPlay app, the logged in account is ours so feel free to use it.

Watching La7

La7 is one of Italy's most commonly watched private channels, it does not have an app but you can watch both on demand and live broadcast through Kodi.

The interface is not super user-friendly, but you just need to open the Kodi app then press on La7-La7d which will be shown as the only Favourite channel.

If you do not know any Italian it will be a bit hard to figure ou what you can watch, but in all honesty most of it would be in Italian anyway, so it might not be of interest to you in general.
